# TL084 — Quad JFET-input op-amp

14-pin DIP. Four general-purpose op-amps in one package, electrically identical to the [TL082](tl082.md) (same JFET-input design, same specs) but with four channels instead of two. Uses the standard quad op-amp pinout (also used by TL074, LM324, MC34074). Supply ±3.5 V to ±18 V dual, or 7 V to 36 V single.

The `TL084IN` in this inventory is the industrial-temperature plastic DIP variant from Texas Instruments — same silicon as the TL084CN / TL084ACN with a slightly wider temperature spec. <!-- lint: ok -->

Datasheet: [TL084](https://www.ti.com/lit/ds/symlink/tl084.pdf)
Octopart: [TL084](https://octopart.com/search?q=TL084)

## ELI5

Same as the [TL082](tl082.md), but with **four** op-amps in one package instead of two. Same input characteristics (JFETs, very high impedance), same supply requirements, same output behaviour.

Reach for it when your circuit has four (or more) op-amp stages — packing them into one chip saves wiring, board space, and one set of decoupling caps compared to two TL082s. Common in audio mixers (one op-amp per channel), multi-stage active filters, and analog computation.

When NOT to: if you only need one or two op-amps, the TL082 is a smaller package and draws less current. The TL084 pulls ~4× the supply current of a TL082.

## At a glance

- **Package**: 14-pin DIP
- **Channels**: 4 op-amps
- **Supply**: ±3.5 V to ±18 V dual, or 7 V to 36 V single
- **Input bias current**: ~30 pA typical
- **Input offset voltage**: ~3 mV typical, ~15 mV max
- **Gain-bandwidth product**: ~3 MHz
- **Slew rate**: ~13 V/µs
- **Output**: not rail-to-rail; swing within ~1.5 V of either rail
- **Supply current**: ~5 mA typical (whole chip)
- **Internally compensated**: stable at unity gain

## Pinout (DIP-14, top view)

```text
      ┌──U──┐
OUT1 1│     │14 OUT4
IN1- 2│     │13 IN4-
IN1+ 3│     │12 IN4+
  V+ 4│     │11 V-
IN2+ 5│     │10 IN3+
IN2- 6│     │ 9 IN3-
OUT2 7│     │ 8 OUT3
      └─────┘
```

Note: V+ is on pin **4**, V- on pin **11** — different positions than the TL082's V+ on pin 8 / V- on pin 4. Easy to swap up when prototyping.

| Pin | Name | Use                              |
| --- | ---- | -------------------------------- |
| 1   | OUT1 | Output of op-amp 1               |
| 2   | IN1- | Inverting input of op-amp 1      |
| 3   | IN1+ | Non-inverting input of op-amp 1  |
| 4   | V+   | Positive supply                  |
| 5   | IN2+ | Non-inverting input of op-amp 2  |
| 6   | IN2- | Inverting input of op-amp 2      |
| 7   | OUT2 | Output of op-amp 2               |
| 8   | OUT3 | Output of op-amp 3               |
| 9   | IN3- | Inverting input of op-amp 3      |
| 10  | IN3+ | Non-inverting input of op-amp 3  |
| 11  | V-   | Negative supply (or GND single)  |
| 12  | IN4+ | Non-inverting input of op-amp 4  |
| 13  | IN4- | Inverting input of op-amp 4      |
| 14  | OUT4 | Output of op-amp 4               |

This is the **standard quad op-amp pinout**, shared with TL074, LM324, MC34074, and most other 14-pin quads. Drop-in replaceable. <!-- lint: ok -->

## Sample circuit — three-input audio mixer

Three input channels each buffered by their own op-amp, then summed by the fourth op-amp as an inverting summer. ±12 V supply:

- **Pin 4 (V+)** → +12 V, with a 100 nF decoupling cap to GND
- **Pin 11 (V-)** → −12 V, with a 100 nF decoupling cap to GND
- **Op-amps 1–3** wired as unity-gain buffers (each output → its own inverting input):
  - Op-amp 1: pin 1 (OUT1) directly to pin 2 (IN1-); signal input on pin 3 (IN1+)
  - Op-amp 2: pin 7 (OUT2) directly to pin 6 (IN2-); signal input on pin 5 (IN2+)
  - Op-amp 3: pin 8 (OUT3) directly to pin 9 (IN3-); signal input on pin 10 (IN3+)
- **Op-amp 4 as inverting summer**:
  - Pin 12 (IN4+) → GND
  - 10 kΩ from each of pins 1, 7, 8 (OUT1, OUT2, OUT3) → pin 13 (IN4-)
  - 10 kΩ from pin 13 (IN4-) → pin 14 (OUT4) (the feedback)
  - Pin 14 (OUT4) → mixed output (inverted; each input arrives at gain = −1)

For a non-inverting mixed output, follow with another inverting stage of gain −1, or use a passive summing network into a non-inverting buffer.

## Variations

- **Multi-pole active filter** → chain two op-amps for a 4th-order Sallen-Key or biquad filter; use all four for an 8th-order stack.
- **Multi-channel buffer** → four independent unity-gain buffers, e.g. for buffering four sensor inputs.
- **Single-supply operation** → tie V- (pin 11) to GND, V+ (pin 4) to single +9 V / +12 V, bias each IN+ to V+/2 via a divider. Needs ≥7 V supply.
- **Instrumentation amplifier** → combine two op-amps with matched resistor networks; use the remaining two for additional stages or a reference buffer.

## Watch out for

- **Supply pin positions differ from the TL082.** V+ on pin 4 (not pin 8), V- on pin 11 (not pin 4). Easy to swap up.
- **Same limits as the TL082**: no rail-to-rail output, ~3 mV offset, minimum ±3.5 V supply, inputs must stay within rails, internally compensated. <!-- lint: ok -->
- **Don't float unused inputs.** Tie them somewhere — even unused channels need defined input voltages.
- **More heat**: ~4× the supply current of a TL082. The package can get warm if all four are driving low-impedance loads at high amplitude.
- **Pin 4 (V+) and pin 11 (V-)** — easy to confuse with the **LM324** (same pinout) or **LM339 comparator** (similar 14-pin layout but different pin functions). Check the datasheet before swapping chips.

## Pairs naturally with

- The [TL082](tl082.md) when only two channels are needed (smaller package, lower current).
- The [7660S](7660s.md) for a negative rail in single-positive-supply designs.
- The **LM6132** in this inventory for rail-to-rail single-supply applications where the TL084 falls short.
- Active filter designs typically use 2–4 op-amps; one TL084 covers most cases.
