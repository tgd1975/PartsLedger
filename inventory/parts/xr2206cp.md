# XR2206CP — Monolithic function generator

16-pin DIP. Produces sine, triangle, and square waves from sub-Hz up to ~1 MHz, with optional amplitude modulation (AM), frequency-shift keying (FSK), and frequency sweep. Set frequency with one resistor and one capacitor.

Datasheet: [XR2206](https://cdn.sparkfun.com/assets/8/a/b/3/9/XR2206.pdf)
Octopart: [XR2206CP](https://octopart.com/search?q=XR2206CP)

## ELI5

Imagine a tiny synthesiser on a chip that always plays exactly one note. You pick: <!-- lint: ok -->

- **The pitch** — by choosing a resistor R and a capacitor C. Roughly, pitch (frequency) = 1 / (R × C). Bigger R or C → lower note.
- **The shape of the note** — square (blocky, like an 8-bit beep), triangle (pointy), or sine (smooth) — selected by how you wire two of the pins.
- **The loudness** — controlled by one pin, which can be driven by another signal to do AM (think tremolo).
- **Two pitches you can hop between** — by swapping in a second timing resistor on a separate pin. That's the FSK feature, originally used for old modems beeping data down a phone line.

So it's a one-note keyboard with shape, volume, and an instant-pitch-jump switch — all in a chip.

What it doesn't do: play melodies, generate noise, or hold a precise frequency over temperature without trimming.

## At a glance

- **Frequency range**: ~0.01 Hz to ~1 MHz (set by R × C, with R typically 4 kΩ–200 kΩ)
- **Supply**: +10 V to +26 V single, or ±12 V dual
- **Sine THD**: ~2.5 % untrimmed; ~0.5 % with both adjust pots
- **Output amplitude (pin 2)**: ~0 to ~6 V p-p, adjustable
- **Outputs**: sine/triangle (pin 2), square sync (pin 15)
- **Modulation**: AM via pin 1, FM/sweep via timing pin current, FSK via pin 9

## Pinout (DIP-16, top view)

```text
         ┌──U──┐
   AMSI 1│     │16 GND
    STO 2│     │15 SYNCO
     MO 3│     │14 WAVEA2
     V+ 4│     │13 WAVEA1
    TC1 5│     │12 SYMA2
    TC2 6│     │11 SYMA1
    TR1 7│     │10 BIAS
    TR2 8│     │9  FSKI
         └─────┘
```

| Pin | Name   | Use                                                       |
| --- | ------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | AMSI   | AM signal input; bias to pin 10 for no modulation         |
| 2   | STO    | Sine or triangle output (AC-coupled through a cap)        |
| 3   | MO     | Multiplier output (alternate output, current mode)        |
| 4   | V+     | Positive supply                                           |
| 5   | TC1    | Timing capacitor (between pins 5 and 6)                   |
| 6   | TC2    | Timing capacitor                                          |
| 7   | TR1    | Timing resistor 1 to GND — sets frequency                 |
| 8   | TR2    | Timing resistor 2 to GND — alternate freq (FSK)           |
| 9   | FSKI   | FSK select: low → use TR1, high → use TR2; or leave open  |
| 10  | BIAS   | Internal reference (~V+/2); use as bias point             |
| 11  | SYMA1  | Sine symmetry trim (200 Ω pot between 11 and 12)          |
| 12  | SYMA2  | Sine symmetry trim                                        |
| 13  | WAVEA1 | Sine shaping (500 Ω pot between 13 and 14 → sine)         |
| 14  | WAVEA2 | Sine shaping; **leave open → triangle output**            |
| 15  | SYNCO  | Square-wave sync output (open collector, needs pull-up)   |
| 16  | GND    | Ground                                                    |

## Sample circuit — single-frequency sine generator

Minimum connections for a ~1 kHz sine wave at ~3 V p-p, +12 V supply:

- **Pin 4 (V+)** → +12 V, with a 0.1 µF decoupling cap to GND
- **Pin 16 (GND)** → ground
- **C (timing)** between pins 5 and 6 → 10 nF
- **R (timing)** from pin 7 to GND → ~100 kΩ (gives f ≈ 1 / (100k × 10n) = 1 kHz)
- **Pin 8** → leave open (no FSK)
- **Pin 9** → leave open (FSK off)
- **Pin 1 (AMSI)** → tie to pin 10 (BIAS) through a resistor (~5.1 kΩ) for fixed amplitude
- **Pin 3 (amplitude set)** → 50 kΩ pot from pin 3 to GND sets output amplitude; or fixed resistor
- **Pin 11–12** → 200 Ω trim pot for sine symmetry
- **Pin 13–14** → 500 Ω trim pot to enable sine shaping (omit = triangle output instead)
- **Pin 2 (STO)** → output through a 1 µF coupling cap to your load
- **Pin 15 (SYNCO)** → 10 kΩ pull-up to V+ if you want the square sync as well; else leave open

Adjust the two trim pots while looking at pin 2 on a scope to minimise distortion.

## Variations

- **Triangle instead of sine** → leave pins 13 and 14 open (skip the 500 Ω pot)
- **Square wave** → take output from pin 15 (sync) with a pull-up to V+
- **FSK (two-tone)** → add a second resistor from pin 8 to GND; pin 9 selects which (low → pin 7, high → pin 8)
- **Sweep/FM** → drive pin 7 or 8 with a varying voltage or current source instead of a fixed resistor
- **Variable frequency** → replace the timing resistor with a pot (keep in 1 kΩ–2 MΩ range)

## Watch out for

- **Timing resistor range**: outside ~1 kΩ–2 MΩ, frequency stability and waveform shape degrade.
- **Timing capacitor**: use a low-leakage type (film, NP0/C0G ceramic). Electrolytics work at low frequencies but drift.
- **Sine quality**: untrimmed sine is visibly distorted (~2.5 % THD). Both adjustment pots are needed to get near the datasheet's 0.5 %.
- **Sync output (pin 15)** is open-collector — it does nothing without a pull-up resistor.
- **No on-chip frequency calibration**: temperature and supply changes shift the output. Not a substitute for a crystal oscillator if frequency precision matters.
- **The chip is long out of production** by the original maker (Exar/MaxLinear). Clones are still sold; quality varies.

## Pairs naturally with

- A scope or audio-in port, for verifying the output before feeding it elsewhere
- An op-amp buffer (TL082, LM6132) if driving anything low-impedance from pin 2
- The 7660S if you only have a +5 V rail and want to run it from a ±5 V supply — though note the XR2206 prefers ≥10 V single-supply for headroom; the 7660S inversion is more useful for the op-amp buffer than for the XR2206 itself
