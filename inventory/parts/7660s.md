# ICL7660S — Charge-pump voltage converter

8-pin DIP. Takes a positive supply and outputs its negation (e.g. +5 V → −5 V), or can be wired as a voltage doubler. Most often used to give op-amps a negative rail when only a single positive supply is available.

Datasheet: [ICL7660S](https://www.renesas.com/en/document/dst/icl7660s-icl7660a-datasheet)
Octopart: [ICL7660S](https://octopart.com/search?q=ICL7660S)

## ELI5

Imagine you've got a 5-volt battery, but your op-amp wants both a +5 and a −5 rail. You don't have a negative battery, and don't want to add a second one.

The 7660S is a tiny "bucket brigade" in a chip. It uses one capacitor as a bucket: first it fills the bucket from the +5 V supply, then it flips the bucket upside down and dumps it into a second capacitor — but flipped. That second capacitor now holds −5 V relative to ground. It does this flip thousands of times per second, so the −5 V output stays topped up.

- **Bucket 1 (C1)**: gets filled, then flipped
- **Bucket 2 (C2)**: catches the flipped charge, smooths it out
- **The chip**: handles the switching; you just supply the buckets

Limits, in the same picture:

- Small buckets, fast switching → only a small current (~20 mA) before the output sags
- The flipping makes a faint "tick tick tick" on the output (switching ripple) — fine for most things, annoying for sensitive audio unless filtered

## At a glance

- **Input range**: ~1.5 V to 12 V (S-variant; original ICL7660 is narrower)
- **Output current**: usable up to ~20 mA before sag is noticeable; no internal regulation
- **External parts**: 2 capacitors, typically 10 µF each
- **Switching freq**: ~10 kHz default; ~35 kHz with BOOST tied high
- **No inductors** — switched-capacitor topology

## Pinout (DIP-8, top view)

```text
        ┌──U──┐
 BOOST 1│     │8 V+
  CAP+ 2│     │7 OSC
   GND 3│     │6 LV
  CAP- 4│     │5 VOUT
        └─────┘
```

| Pin | Name  | Use                                                          |
| --- | ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1   | BOOST | Raises oscillator frequency; usually leave open              |
| 2   | CAP+  | Pump capacitor, positive side                                |
| 3   | GND   | Ground                                                       |
| 4   | CAP-  | Pump capacitor, negative side                                |
| 5   | VOUT  | Output (≈ −V+ in inverter config)                            |
| 6   | LV    | Tie to GND when V+ < ~3.5 V, else leave open                 |
| 7   | OSC   | Slow oscillator with external cap; usually leave open        |
| 8   | V+    | Positive supply input                                        |

## Sample circuit — voltage inverter (+V → −V)

Minimum connections:

- **Pin 8 (V+)** → input supply (e.g. +5 V)
- **Pin 3 (GND)** → ground
- **C1** between pin 2 (CAP+) and pin 4 (CAP-) — 10 µF; if electrolytic, + side to pin 2
- **C2** between pin 5 (VOUT) and GND — 10 µF; if electrolytic, **− side to pin 5** (VOUT is negative)
- **Pin 6 (LV)** — leave open for V+ ≥ ~3.5 V, tie to GND below that
- **Pins 1 (BOOST) and 7 (OSC)** — leave open

Output at pin 5 sits at approximately −V+, with magnitude dropping under load.

## Watch out for

- **Output sag**: not a regulated supply. Drawing > ~20 mA visibly reduces the output magnitude. Pair with a low-dropout regulator on the output if a stable rail matters.
- **Switching ripple** at the oscillator frequency. Audible-band content is possible at the default ~10 kHz — filter at the load, or use BOOST to push ripple above audio.
- **LV pin**: leaving it open at low V+ can produce no output. Easy to miss.
- **Capacitor type**: ceramic is safe for general use; electrolytics work but watch polarity (especially C2).
