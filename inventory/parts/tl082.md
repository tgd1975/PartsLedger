# TL082 — Dual JFET-input op-amp

8-pin DIP. Two general-purpose op-amps in one package, with JFET inputs that draw almost no current (typical bias ~30 pA). Uses the standard dual op-amp pinout (also used by TL072, NE5532, LM358, OPA2134). Supply ±3.5 V to ±18 V dual, or 7 V to 36 V single.

Both `TL082CF` and `TL082CP` in this inventory are marking variants of the same chip — different package suffixes from Texas Instruments, identical silicon.

Datasheet: [TL082](https://www.ti.com/lit/ds/symlink/tl082.pdf)
Octopart: [TL082](https://octopart.com/search?q=TL082)

## ELI5

An **op-amp** is a tiny amplifier on a chip. You give it two inputs (call them `+` and `−`) and it outputs the difference between them, multiplied by a huge number (~100 000). On its own that's not useful — but when you wire up resistors around it, the multiplication settles to whatever the resistors say, and you've built a precise amplifier or filter.

The TL082 is special because its inputs are **JFETs** — transistors that let almost no current flow into them. So it works well with high-impedance signals (a guitar pickup, a piezo sensor, a photodiode) where a regular op-amp would load the source down and degrade the signal.

The "dual" part: there are **two** complete op-amps in this 8-pin package, sharing the same power pins. Cheaper and smaller than two separate single op-amps when you need a stereo or two-stage circuit.

What it's good at: audio, sensor front-ends, active filters, sample-and-hold.

What it's not good at: precision DC (offset voltage is a few mV, too much for instrumentation), rail-to-rail output (the output stops ~1.5 V short of either rail), low-voltage single-supply (needs ≥7 V single or ±3.5 V dual — won't run from 3.3 V or 5 V single).

## At a glance

- **Package**: 8-pin DIP (also SOIC; this inventory is through-hole)
- **Channels**: 2 op-amps
- **Supply**: ±3.5 V to ±18 V dual, or 7 V to 36 V single
- **Input bias current**: ~30 pA typical (very low — JFET inputs)
- **Input offset voltage**: ~3 mV typical, ~15 mV max — not a precision part
- **Gain-bandwidth product**: ~3 MHz
- **Slew rate**: ~13 V/µs
- **Output**: not rail-to-rail; swing within ~1.5 V of either rail
- **Supply current**: ~2.5 mA typical (whole chip)
- **Internally compensated**: stable at unity gain, no compensation cap needed

## Pinout (DIP-8, top view)

```text
      ┌──U──┐
OUT1 1│     │8 V+
IN1- 2│     │7 OUT2
IN1+ 3│     │6 IN2-
  V- 4│     │5 IN2+
      └─────┘
```

| Pin | Name | Use                                                      |
| --- | ---- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | OUT1 | Output of op-amp 1                                       |
| 2   | IN1- | Inverting input of op-amp 1                              |
| 3   | IN1+ | Non-inverting input of op-amp 1                          |
| 4   | V-   | Negative supply (or GND in single-supply mode)           |
| 5   | IN2+ | Non-inverting input of op-amp 2                          |
| 6   | IN2- | Inverting input of op-amp 2                              |
| 7   | OUT2 | Output of op-amp 2                                       |
| 8   | V+   | Positive supply                                          |

This is the **standard dual op-amp pinout**, shared with LM358, TL072, NE5532, OPA2134, and most other 8-pin duals. You can usually drop in a different dual op-amp without changing the board — supply, bandwidth and offset specs differ but pin assignments match. <!-- lint: ok -->

## Sample circuit — unity-gain buffer (voltage follower)

The simplest useful op-amp circuit: a buffer that copies its input to its output without loading the input. Common as an impedance-matching stage for high-impedance sources (guitar pickup, electret mic, photodiode) feeding into a lower-impedance next stage.

Minimum connections for op-amp 1 as a buffer, ±12 V supply:

- **Pin 8 (V+)** → +12 V, with a 100 nF decoupling cap to GND
- **Pin 4 (V-)** → −12 V, with a 100 nF decoupling cap to GND
- **Pin 3 (IN1+)** → signal input (very high impedance — the op-amp loads the source with ~10¹² Ω)
- **Pin 1 (OUT1)** wired directly to **Pin 2 (IN1-)** (this is the feedback that fixes gain = 1) <!-- lint: ok -->
- **Pin 1 (OUT1)** → buffered output to the next stage

The second op-amp (pins 5/6/7) can be used independently or left unused — if unused, tie inputs to ground (or any defined voltage) and leave the output open. **Don't leave inputs floating.**

## Variations

- **Non-inverting amplifier** → add R1 from IN1- (pin 2) to GND and R2 from IN1- (pin 2) to OUT1 (pin 1). Gain = 1 + R2/R1. Example: R1 = 1 kΩ, R2 = 10 kΩ → gain = 11.
- **Inverting amplifier** → signal in through R1 to IN1- (pin 2); IN1+ (pin 3) to GND; R2 from pin 2 to pin 1. Gain = −R2/R1. Inverts polarity.
- **Active filter** → replace R2 with a capacitor (integrator) or an RC network (low-pass / high-pass / band-pass) for filter behaviour.
- **Single-supply operation** → tie V- (pin 4) to GND, V+ (pin 8) to a single +9 V or +12 V supply, and bias IN+ to V+/2 via a resistor divider so the signal can swing around mid-rail. Needs ≥7 V supply.
- **Stereo channel pair** → use op-amp 1 for left, op-amp 2 for right.

## Watch out for

- **Not for low-voltage single-supply.** If you only have +5 V or +3.3 V, the TL082 won't work — minimum is ~7 V single or ±3.5 V dual. Use a rail-to-rail single-supply part (LM6132 or LM358 in this inventory) instead.
- **Output doesn't reach the rails.** On ±12 V supply, expect ~±10.5 V output swing. If you need rail-to-rail, the **LM6132** in this inventory does the job for many cases.
- **Offset voltage is high** (3–15 mV) for precision DC. Fine for audio; not fine for measuring small DC voltages without a chopper or auto-zero amplifier.
- **Inputs must stay within the supply rails.** Driving an input above V+ or below V- can cause phase inversion or latch-up. <!-- lint: ok -->
- **Don't float unused inputs.** Tie them somewhere — to GND, to V+/2, or via a resistor to a defined potential.
- **Decoupling matters.** 100 nF ceramic close to each supply pin; for audio, add 10 µF electrolytics on the board's supply rails.

## Pairs naturally with

- The [TL084](tl084.md) when you need four channels instead of two (same chip family, just quad in DIP-14).
- The [7660S](7660s.md) to generate a negative rail from a single positive supply, when running the TL082 in dual-supply mode from a single battery.
- The **LM6132** in this inventory for rail-to-rail single-supply applications where the TL082 can't reach the rails.
- The **LM358** for non-audio single-supply use where ~700 kHz bandwidth and a single rail are enough.
- DC-blocking capacitors on audio signal paths (rule of thumb: 1–10 µF series cap on input and output).
